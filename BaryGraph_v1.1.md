# BaryGraph
## A CM-Topology Knowledge Graph Architecture
**Version 1.2 · April 2026**

> **v1.2 changes from v1.1:**
> - Forest structure with unique-parent constraint. Every CM has at most
>   one `parent_edge_id`. Orphans allowed.
> - Lateral MetaBary removed. All MetaBary connections are upward triads.
> - `is_metabary`, `hierarchy_direction`, `common_ancestor_id` fields
>   removed — redundant with level inspection and forest structure.
> - `v(type)` is now level-dependent: per-pair contextual embeddings at
>   leaf levels, fixed TYPE_SENTENCES at intermediate levels, bridge
>   vectors at MetaBary levels.
> - Word/concept vectors computed as BE-centroid of children, not raw
>   embedding centroid.
> - MetaBary formula: `normalize(q_MB·v(BE₁) + q_MB·v(BE₂) + (1-q_MB)·v(bridge))`
>   with rescaled `q_MB = q₃ / √(q₁² + q₂² + q₃²)`.
> - Level orientation clarified: L1 = top (most abstract), L15 = bottom
>   (most concrete).

---

## 1. Motivation

Standard knowledge graphs treat relationships as thin directed edges:
label + weight between two nodes. Standard vector databases treat
documents as points in embedding space, retrieved by cosine similarity.
Both approaches lose relational structure that is often the most valuable
part of a corpus.

Consider two papers: one reporting an unexplained spacecraft flyby anomaly
in planetary science, another reporting an anomalous orbital residual in
stellar dynamics. Neither cites the other. Their vectors are distant in
embedding space. Standard retrieval returns neither when querying the
other. Yet they describe the same underlying physical phenomenon at
different scales.

BaryGraph makes that connection first-class and queryable. The
relationship between the two papers is not a thin edge — it is a document
with its own vector, its own properties, and its own position in the
hierarchy. It can be retrieved directly, traversed, and used to discover
further bridges.

## 2. Core Concepts

### 2.1 The Triad as Atomic Unit

Every connection in BaryGraph is a triad of three entities:

```
CM₁  →  BaryEdge  →  CM₂
```

CM₁ and CM₂ are knowledge nodes (papers, concepts, domains, authors). The
BaryEdge is the relationship document sitting between them. It is not a
pointer — it is a first-class stored object with its own embedding vector,
its own properties, and its own identity. It can be retrieved, queried,
and promoted to a CM role in MetaBary connections.

### 2.2 Hierarchy Levels

All nodes and BaryEdges carry a `level` property (1–15). L1 = top (most
abstract), L15 = bottom (most concrete). The level determines the scale
at which a connection operates:

| Level | Scale | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| 1–3 | Paradigm / Theory | Physics, Biology, Computer Science |
| 4–6 | Field / Sub-field | Astrophysics, Molecular Biology, NLP |
| 7–9 | Mechanism / Method | Orbital mechanics, CRISPR, Transformers |
| 10–12 | Topic cluster | Flyby anomalies, gene regulation, attention |
| 13–14 | Individual document / word | Specific papers, dictionary entries |
| 15 | Entity / Claim / Sense | Named measurement, specific claim, word sense |

**BaryEdges connect two nodes at the same level (horizontal).**
Cross-level relationships are handled by MetaBary triads (see §2.4).

### 2.3 The BaryEdge Vector

Every BaryEdge has an embedding vector computed as a weighted mixture of
its two CM vectors plus a relationship-type vector:

```
bary_vec = normalize( q·v(CM₁) + q·v(CM₂) + (1−q)·v(type) )
```

where `q` is connection quality (0–1) and `v(type)` is level-dependent:

- **Leaf levels (L14–L15):** per-pair contextual embeddings constructed
  from the lexical/semantic neighborhood of both CMs. Much richer than
  a single generic sentence.
- **Intermediate levels:** fixed natural-language TYPE_SENTENCES per
  edge type.
- **MetaBary levels (L13 and above):** the bridge BaryEdge's vector
  serves directly as the third component — no embedding call needed.

A strong connection (`q → 1`) produces a barycenter close to both CM
vectors simultaneously. A weak connection (`q → 0`) tilts the barycenter
toward the semantic space of the relationship type itself.

The BaryEdge also stores a `registry.summary` — a short natural-language
description of what the connection means, generated by an LLM at
ingestion time. This summary is embedded as a separate `summary_vector`
field, providing a second retrieval signal independent of the structural
`bary_vec`.

### 2.4 MetaBary: Triadic Recursion

Above the base matching levels (L14/L15), BaryEdges act as CMs for new
connections. Two BaryEdges at the same level are connected via a third
(bridge) BaryEdge from one level below, forming a **MetaBary triad**:

```
BE₁ (level L)  →  MetaBary (level L-2)  →  BE₂ (level L)
                      ↑ bridge
                  BE (level L-1)
```

MetaBary vector:

```
meta_bary = normalize( q_MB·v(BE₁) + q_MB·v(BE₂) + (1−q_MB)·v(bridge) )
q_MB      = q₃ / √(q₁² + q₂² + q₃²)
```

where `q₁`, `q₂` are the connection strengths of `BE₁` and `BE₂`, and
`q₃` is the strength of the bridge. The rescaling ensures `q_MB` accounts
for the quality of all three inputs.

**Forest structure:** The unique-parent constraint (`parent_edge_id` on
every CM) guarantees a forest topology. Every node or BaryEdge has at
most one parent MetaBary. Orphans are allowed — a CM with no parent
simply has no upward path. This is correct behavior: a concept with no
relationships has no place in the abstraction hierarchy.

**Why this replaces HierarchyLinks and lateral MetaBary:**

HierarchyLinks were a special-purpose connector that broke the
fundamental rule: every connection is a triad. Lateral MetaBary
(same-level cross-paradigm connections) created cycles and required
`common_ancestor_id` validation. The forest structure eliminates both:

- Cross-level hierarchy is encoded naturally by the level gap between
  MetaBary and its children.
- Cross-paradigm insight emerges when two BaryEdges from different
  domains share a bridge, rather than requiring an explicit lateral
  connector.
- Single `$graphLookup` on `parent_edge_id` walks from any node to root.

### 2.5 BE-Centroid Vectors

Intermediate node vectors (e.g., word nodes containing multiple senses)
are computed as the centroid of their children's BaryEdge vectors, not
raw embedding centroids:

```
v(node) = normalize( Σ v(BE_i) + Σ v(orphan_child_j) )
```

This absorbs relational information from the pairings below. A node
whose children paired with diverse partners gets a vector in the
subspace where those relational neighborhoods overlap — exactly the
property needed for effective bridging at the next MetaBary level.

## 3. Data Schema

All documents live in a single MongoDB collection. The `doc_type` field
distinguishes entity nodes from BaryEdges.

### 3.1 CM Node

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| `_id` | ObjectId — primary key |
| `doc_type` | `"node"` |
| `node_type` | `"paper"` \| `"domain"` \| `"concept"` \| `"author"` \| `"institution"` \| `"paradigm"` |
| `level` | Integer 1–15 |
| `label` | Human-readable name or title |
| `vector` | Float array — embedding or BE-centroid |
| `surface` | Integer — maximum BaryEdges this node can sustain |
| `rotation` | Float — update frequency |
| `parent_edge_id` | ObjectId \| null — at most one parent BE/MetaBary |
| `properties` | Type-specific payload |
| `created_at` / `updated_at` | Dates |

### 3.2 BaryEdge

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| `_id` | ObjectId — primary key |
| `doc_type` | `"baryedge"` |
| `cm1_id` | ObjectId → CM Node or BaryEdge |
| `cm2_id` | ObjectId → CM Node or BaryEdge |
| `level` | Same as CMs at leaf levels; `cm1.level - 2` for MetaBary |
| `edge_type` | Relationship type (leaf levels only; null above) |
| `q` | Float 0–1 — connection quality |
| `connection_strength` | Float — q or q_MB |
| `vector` | Float array — `bary_vec` (structural position signal) |
| `summary_vector` | Float array — embedding of `registry.summary` (leaf levels) |
| `type_vector` | Float array — `v(type)` used in bary_vec computation |
| `registry` | `{ shared_concepts, divergence, summary }` (leaf levels) |
| `parent_edge_id` | ObjectId \| null — at most one parent MetaBary |
| `source` | `"ingested"` \| `"manual"` \| `"inferred"` |
| `confidence` | Float 0–1 |
| `created_at` / `updated_at` | Dates |

**Above leaf levels:** only structural fields are needed (`_id`,
`doc_type`, `cm1_id`, `cm2_id`, `level`, `vector`, `parent_edge_id`,
`connection_strength`, timestamps). Registry, summary_vector, edge_type,
and type_vector are meaningful only at the levels where explicit
relations are matched.

## 4. Ingestion Pipeline

### 4.1 Base Nodes (leaf levels)

Each document is embedded and stored as a CM Node. Fine-grained entities
(word senses, specific claims) at L15; broader entries (words, papers)
at L14.

### 4.2 BaryEdge Formation (Bottom-Up)

**Leaf-level matching (L14–L15):**

1. At L15: cosine-driven greedy matching of all node pairs above
   threshold. v(type) = per-pair contextual embedding.
2. Compute intermediate node vectors (BE-centroid + orphan children).
3. At L14: relation-driven matching using corpus structure. v(type) =
   TYPE_SENTENCES per edge type. Fermion order: rarer relations matched
   first to preserve high-value signals.
4. Orphan re-entry at both levels.

**MetaBary formation (L13 and above):**

5. For each L14 BE (bridge): find two unparented L15 BEs with mutual
   cosine above threshold → form L13 MetaBary.
6. Repeat recursively upward until no new triads form.
7. LLM summaries generated asynchronously for leaf-level BaryEdges.

### 4.3 Cluster Nodes (intermediate levels)

Domain/cluster nodes at L10–L6 are created by clustering BaryEdges from
the level below. The cluster node vector is the centroid of its
children's BaryEdge vectors.

## 5. Query Patterns

### 5.1 Standard Retrieval

Vector search across all `doc_type` values simultaneously. Returns a mix
of nodes and BaryEdges ranked by relevance.

### 5.2 Relationship-Aware Retrieval

```js
db.barygraph.aggregate([
  { $vectorSearch: {
      index: 'barygraph_vector', path: 'vector',
      queryVector: embed('query text'),
      numCandidates: 200, limit: 20,
      filter: { doc_type: { $in: ['node', 'baryedge'] }, level: 15 }
  }},
  { $lookup: { from: 'barygraph', localField: 'cm1_id', foreignField: '_id', as: 'cm1' }},
  { $lookup: { from: 'barygraph', localField: 'cm2_id', foreignField: '_id', as: 'cm2' }}
])
```

### 5.3 Cross-Domain Bridge Query

```js
filter: { edge_type: 'same_phenomenon' }
```

Returns only BaryEdges bridging different domains. Each result implies
both parent nodes as context.

### 5.4 Hierarchy Traversal (Forest Walk)

```js
db.barygraph.aggregate([
  { $match: { _id: startNodeId }},
  { $graphLookup: {
      from: 'barygraph',
      startWith: '$parent_edge_id',
      connectFromField: 'parent_edge_id',
      connectToField: '_id',
      as: 'upward_chain',
      maxDepth: 15
  }},
  { $project: {
      chain: { $sortArray: { input: '$upward_chain', sortBy: { level: 1 } } }
  }}
])
```

Forest structure means no cycle handling needed. Single `$graphLookup`
on `parent_edge_id` walks from any node to root.

## 6. Key Architectural Rules

- **BaryEdges** connect two CMs at the **same level** (horizontal).
- **MetaBary** connects two BaryEdges at the same level using a bridge
  from one level below. This is the only cross-level mechanism.
- There are **no HierarchyLinks** and **no lateral MetaBary**. The
  triad topology and forest structure are universal.
- **Unique parent (soft):** every CM has at most one `parent_edge_id`.
  Orphans are allowed.
- A node with no BaryEdges at its level has no upward MetaBary path.
  This is correct: a concept with no relationships has no hierarchy
  position.
- `bary_vec` is always computed from CM vectors algebraically — never
  embedded directly. If a CM vector updates, its BaryEdge vectors must
  be recomputed.
- `summary_vector` and `bary_vec` are kept separate. The evaluation
  measures each signal independently before any merge decision.
- `v(type)` construction is level-dependent: per-pair contextual
  embeddings at leaf levels, TYPE_SENTENCES at intermediate levels,
  bridge vectors at MetaBary levels.
- Connection quality `q` decays with staleness:
  `q(t) = q₀ · exp(−λ·Δt)`

## 7. Infrastructure

Single MongoDB collection. Vector search index on the `vector` field
covers all `doc_type` values simultaneously.

```json
{
  "fields": [
    { "type": "vector", "path": "vector", "numDimensions": 768, "similarity": "cosine" },
    { "type": "filter", "path": "doc_type" },
    { "type": "filter", "path": "level" },
    { "type": "filter", "path": "edge_type" },
    { "type": "filter", "path": "node_type" }
  ]
}
```

Embedding dimensions are corpus-dependent (768 for short-text corpora
like dictionaries; 1536 for longer documents).

## 9. Open Questions

1. Optimal cosine threshold for MetaBary triad formation — 0.9 is
   the current default; tune per corpus.
2. Whether `summary_vector` should be a separate mongot index or a second
   vector path in the primary index.
3. Whether cluster node vectors (centroids of child BaryEdge vectors)
   should include MetaBary vectors in the centroid computation.
4. Formal evaluation metric for MetaBary correctness — no standard
   ground truth exists for triadic hierarchy.
5. MetaBary stopping criterion — "no triads form at a level" (natural)
   vs. hard cap.
6. Sparsity above L12 — each level roughly halves count with cos > 0.9
   threshold. Graph may top out well before L1.
